How To Cook Steak On The Stovetop

Description

With just a handful of ingredients and a single pan, you can prepare a steak that rivals anything youâ€™d enjoy at a high-end steakhouse.

Ingredients

  • 2 New York strip or ribeye steaks
  • 1 heaping teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • A few sprigs fresh thyme leaves

Steps

  1. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels, then season all over with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a heavy pan (preferably cast-iron or stainless steel) over medium-high heat until very hot, then add oil and heat until it begins to shimmer and move fluidly in the pan.
  3. Carefully set the steaks in the pan, releasing them away from you so that the oil doesn't splatter in your direction.
  4. Leave the steaks alone to develop a golden crust. Flip the steaks when they release easily and the bottom is a deep-brown color, about 3 minutes.
  5. Continue to cook the steaks for another 3 to 4 minutes for medium rare. For medium, cook for 4 to 5 minutes. For well-done, cook for 5 to 6 minutes.
  6. During the last minute of cooking, add the butter and thyme sprigs to the pan with the steaks.
  7. If serving the steaks unsliced, transfer them to plates and serve hot. If you plan to slice the steaks, transfer them to a cutting board and cover with aluminum foil. Let steaks rest for 5 to 10 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
